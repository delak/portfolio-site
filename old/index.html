Final thesis project for my Masters. Stockpile forecasts the landscape of the Internet of Things (IoT). Check out the full Stockpile synthesis site. My goal was to approach the IoT from a User Experience point of view. The research dealt with its feasibility, desirability and viability. Stockpile also explores other effects that the widespread use of IoT technology can have on supply chain. The challenges posed are two-fold; enabling large-scale and bottom-up creation of smart products, and consumer information overload. The web-based interface functions as both a personal IoT and personal supply chain management tool of sorts. Icon badges and rollover panels allow quick access to notifications from object profiles... ...and allow users to interact directly with all sorts of objects with different types of sensors. Here, the user is able to review their energy consumption for previous loads of laundry, and adjust their behavior accordingly. Stockpile facilitates different business models. The object profile is a two-way communication platform between service provider and consumer. Third-party service providers of all kinds can plug into the Stockpile system.
